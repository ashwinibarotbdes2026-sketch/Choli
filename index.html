Visual Identity

Illustrated Identity as Storytelling

The brand identity is built around the idea of translating the four founders into fictional illustrated personas. Rather than representing ourselves literally, we construct a visual world where our dynamic, humour, and shared experiences can be exaggerated, reinterpreted, and documented over time.

This approach allows the brand to function as an evolving narrative system. The characters become vehicles for storytelling, capturing everyday interactions, inside jokes, and group dynamics in a way that is both personal and scalable.

The intention is not to create mascots, but to build a living visual language; one that grows with the brand and reflects the relationship between the four founders.

The Intimate Layer

A spontaneous doodle created during a shared outing.

Vision

To reposition lingerie from a concealed, disposable underlayer to a visible, durable, and materially conscious garment, rooted in indigenous cotton systems such as organic cotton and kala cotton.

Mission

  1. To develop a category of non-synthetic, repairable intimate wear using rain-fed, low-input fibres (kala cotton) and certified organic cotton.

  2. To challenge the dominant aesthetic of invisibility in lingerie by legitimising the visibility of bra straps as a styling language.

  3. To extend garment life cycles through design-for-repair frameworks, including user manuals and brand-supported mending systems.

  4. To embed craft practices (block printing) within contemporary intimate wear without reducing them to surface-level ornamentation.

Problem

Intimate wear constitutes a disproportionately high-waste category due to:

Non-recyclable, hygiene constraints.

Decades to centuries.

Our Design Philosophy

01

Visibility as Intent

Straps are designed to be seen, not hidden. Lingerie operates as a layered garment, not a base layer.

Cultural reference point:The "free the tits" movement — body autonomy through exposure."Show them straps" — garment autonomy through visibility.
02

Anti-Synthetic Construction

No elastane-heavy dependency (or minimised strategically). Fabric-led support rather than engineered compression. Soft structuring through cut, ties, and layering — not wires.

03

Repairability

Modular components (straps, bands, cups separable where possible). Reinforced stress points.

Repair Ecosystem: Brand repair service (warranty based).

Packaging

The packaging is designed as a modular, reusable fabric system made from upcycled textile waste. Instead of functioning as single-use packaging, it transforms into multiple utility formats such as a tote, mini organiser, or tiffin bag through a system of folds, loops, and buttons.

A QR code-based illustrated manual guides the user through these transformations, making the interaction intuitive and engaging. This approach extends the lifecycle of packaging, reduces waste, and shifts it from a disposable element to a functional, long-term object.

The design aligns with the brand's larger philosophy of product circularity and material consciousness, positioning packaging as an active part of the product experience.

Illustration System

Route One

Refined Cultural Illustration

This direction draws from traditional Indian feminine illustration styles, where figures are typically depicted as poised, self-contained, and visually balanced. The aesthetic is deliberate — controlled linework, stylised features, and a sense of stillness.

Within this framework, the characters are positioned as composed and nonchalant. However, this sense of control is intentionally disrupted through their interactions. Their poses, proximity, and gestures introduce moments of unexpected intimacy, absurdity, or subtle chaos.

This contrast creates a layered reading: while the figures appear calm and refined individually, their collective behaviour reveals a more unfiltered, unrestrained dynamic.

Route Two

Doodle Archive

This direction originates from a real, documented moment — a spontaneous doodle created during a shared outing. The drawing captures the four founders through simplified, exaggerated forms, translating personality into instinctive marks and gestures.

Unlike the constructed nature of the refined illustrations, this style is direct and unmediated. The lines are uneven, the proportions are inconsistent, and the expressions are exaggerated — reflecting a sense of ease, familiarity, and informality within the group.

This visual language operates as a personal archive. It represents how the founders perceive each other in private — playful, chaotic, and unselfconscious.

Within the brand system, Route Two functions as an intimate layer. It complements the more composed illustration style by grounding the brand in lived experience.

Marketing and Communication Strategy · 5 P's of Marketing

प्र

Product

  • Functionality: wire-free support using knitted cotton structures, allowing natural stretch and recovery without elastane dependency.
  • Incorporates padding for support.
  • Hook and eye closures similar to traditional bras.
  • Appearance: visible lingerie (straps as styling element), minimal, culturally rooted.
  • Quality: kala cotton + organic cotton — breathable, skin-safe, durable.
  • Warranty: repair-based system (brand-supported mending).
  • Packaging: modular, reusable fabric packaging aligned with anti-waste principles.
मू

Price

  • Premium, value-based strategy, justified through material integrity, longevity, and system design.
  • Higher than mass lingerie brands (₹800–₹2,000).
  • Comparable to or slightly above premium sustainable brands.
  • Justified through: non-synthetic material system, knitted construction innovation, built-in repair ecosystem, extended lifecycle.
  • Price range: ₹3,100–₹3,300.
प्र

Promotion

  • Advertising: concept-led visuals using illustrated identity and character-based storytelling.
  • Public relations: engagement with themes of sustainability, repair culture, anti-synthetic materials, and lingerie visibility.
  • Message: "Show Them Straps".
  • Media: Instagram (primary storytelling platform), website.
  • Pop-ups, exhibitions (e.g. Lil Flea, Raw Collaborative), and design-led retail environments.
स्थ

Place

  • Distribution channels: D2C (website) for full brand experience and storytelling.
  • Stockists / curated retail partners for physical access and discovery.
  • Logistics: small-batch or made-to-order production.
  • Service levels: integrated repair services as part of after-sales engagement.
  • Location: urban design-conscious markets (Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi).
  • Market coverage: niche, selectively expanded through aligned stockists (Good Earth).
